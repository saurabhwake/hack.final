"Urban Planning Tool Web" could refer to a web-based urban planning tool, typically a software application or platform accessible through a web browser. These tools leverage the capabilities of the internet and cloud computing to provide urban planners, architects, policymakers, and other stakeholders with convenient access to planning resources and functionalities from anywhere with an internet connection. Here are some key features and characteristics of web-based urban planning tools: Data Integration: Many web-based urban planning tools offer capabilities for integrating and analyzing various types of data relevant to urban planning, such as spatial data (GIS layers), demographic data, transportation data, environmental data, and socioeconomic indicators. Visualization: Visualization is a key aspect of urban planning, and web-based tools often provide advanced visualization capabilities for creating maps, charts, graphs, and 3D models to communicate planning concepts and scenarios effectively
